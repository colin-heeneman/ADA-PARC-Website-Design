Working-Age People Living in Poverty

Published by ADA-PARC — September 2025

ada

The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) rests on the idea that people with disabilities should be assured equal opportunity and economic self-sufficiency.1 Yet, people with disabilities remain over twice as likely to live in poverty.2

Despite the ADA's call for economic freedom and equal opportunity, some states are doing better than others to ensure that people with disabilities are not living in poverty. The maps and tables below display state percentages for working-aged people with disabilities (ages 18–64) who are below the poverty line. Higher percentages mean more disabled people are living below the poverty line.

The strain of economic hardship highlights the ways in which disability and poverty remain intertwined. People with disabilities make up less than 13% of the United States population, but over 50% of its people live in long-term poverty.3 Every state has the responsibility to ensure people with disabilities can live free from poverty.
