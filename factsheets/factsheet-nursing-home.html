Rates of Nursing Home Institutionalization
Among People with Disabilities Ages 18 to 64

Published by ADA-PARC, September 2025

ada

The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) mandates the right to live in the community with supports instead of living in a nursing home. The U.S. Supreme Court upheld this right in the landmark Olmstead decision in 1999.1

Despite this right to live in the community, some states are doing worse on overall nursing home rates, and some are doing better to support people with disabilities to live in the community. The map and tables below display state percentages for working-age people with disabilities (ages 18–64) residing in nursing homes.

Higher percentages mean more people with disabilities (PWD) are living in nursing homes. Lower percentages mean fewer people in nursing homes and more PWD living in the community.2

State Map

Note on map color direction: For this indicator, darker colors indicate higher nursing home rates (worse performance, more institutionalization), and lighter colors indicate lower rates (better performance, more community living).
